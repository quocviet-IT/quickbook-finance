## 1. Technology Requirements

### Preferred Browser

The manual identifies **Google Chrome** as the preferred browser for QuickBooks Online. It also states that QuickBooks Online works with:

- Safari;
- Firefox.

Recommended setup:

1. Bookmark the QuickBooks Online login page.
2. Keep the login username available on the devices used for access.
3. Use a stable internet connection and a supported browser.

### Working with Multiple QuickBooks Accounts

One browser session is normally sufficient. When a user needs to work with multiple QuickBooks accounts, separate Chrome profiles can help keep sessions isolated.

#### Create a Separate Chrome Profile

1. Open Chrome.
2. Select the profile or **People** button in the upper-right corner.
3. Select **Manage people**.
4. Select **Add person**.
5. Choose a profile photo and name.
6. Select **Save**.
7. Sign in to the new Chrome profile with a Google Account.

Source reference from the manual: `bit.ly/qbochromemultiuser`

> **Practical use:** Separate browser profiles can reduce accidental entry into the wrong company file when staff manage more than one QuickBooks business.

## 2. Introduction to QuickBooks Online

QuickBooks Online is a cloud accounting product intended to support different small-business operating models. The source manual highlights three broad categories.

### 2.1 Project-Based Businesses

Businesses paid by project, job, or engagement, including examples such as:

- contractors;
- photographers;
- wedding planners.

Typical system needs include customer records, estimates or quotes, invoicing, project-related income, expenses, and payment tracking.

### 2.2 Appointment-Based Businesses

Businesses that deliver services by appointment, including examples such as:

- medical practices;
- salons;
- driving schools.

Typical system needs include customer billing, service items, recurring costs, payment collection, and reporting.

### 2.3 Retail and eCommerce Businesses

Businesses that sell or resell products, including examples such as:

- food trucks;
- online marketplace sellers;
- coffee shops.

Typical system needs include products and services, sales, inventory where supported, expenses, bank transactions, and tax tracking.

### Suitability Questions

Before selecting QuickBooks Online, confirm:

- whether the business sells services, products, or both;
- whether inventory tracking is required;
- how many business users need direct access;
- whether an accountant or bookkeeper needs access;
- whether projects, classes, locations, payroll, multi-currency, or purchase orders are required;
- whether the product supports the business's country, tax rules, banks, and compliance requirements.

## 4. Benefits of Using QuickBooks Online

The manual emphasizes the following cloud-accounting benefits.

### 4.1 Mobile Access

Users can create and access accounting records from supported mobile devices. The manual highlights activities such as:

- creating a quote;
- emailing an invoice;
- recording an expense;
- working from different locations and time zones.

### 4.2 Real-Time Collaboration

Cloud access enables business users, accountants, bookkeepers, and team members to work with the same company information without manually exchanging local data files.

Potential benefits include:

- faster review and correction;
- less file duplication;
- improved coordination;
- more timely management decisions.

### 4.3 Backup and Security

The manual presents automatic cloud backup and security as major benefits of online accounting.

> **Control note:** Cloud backup does not remove the need for access reviews, strong authentication, export procedures, retention policies, and recovery testing.

### 4.4 Reduced Capital Expenditure

Because the software is hosted online, the business does not need to manage the same type of local application upgrade process associated with traditional desktop installations.

The manual notes that cloud software updates can be applied automatically.

### 4.5 Improved Agility and Productivity

Cloud accounting can reduce time spent on:

- manual file sharing;
- duplicate data entry;
- administrative coordination;
- maintaining local software versions.

This can allow staff to spend more time on core business activities.

## 5. Mobile Devices and QuickBooks Online

The source manual states that QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Online Accountant can be accessed on mobile devices supported by:

- iOS;
- Android.

The manual encourages learners to download the QuickBooks Online mobile application from the relevant app store for mobile demonstrations during training.

### Recommended Mobile Controls

For business use, apply at least the following controls:

- require device screen locks;
- enable multi-factor authentication where available;
- avoid shared personal accounts;
- review user permissions;
- remove access from lost or replaced devices;
- avoid storing sensitive exports in unsecured local folders.

## 6. Training Business Case: Zippy Electrical & Data Services Pty Ltd

The manual uses a fictional or training case involving **Damien Chow** and **Zippy Electrical & Data Services Pty Ltd**.

### 6.1 Tax and Reporting Context

The business case states that the company:

- prepares a Business Activity Statement (BAS) quarterly;
- prepares an Instalment Activity Statement (IAS) monthly;
- pays AUD 2,500 in PAYG instalments quarterly;
- uses the cash method for GST;
- is required to lodge a Taxable Payments Annual Report (TPAR) annually.

### 6.2 Payroll and Workforce

The business has:

- Damien as the owner;
- one employee on payroll;
- additional workers treated as contractors;
- a requirement to pay superannuation through QuickBooks.

### 6.3 User Access

The case requires access for:

- Damien;
- one employee performing bookkeeping work;
- one BAS agent assisting with bookkeeping and BAS lodgements;
- one accountant assisting with end-of-financial-year returns.

### 6.4 Migration Requirements

Damien is migrating from another accounting package and already has:

- customers;
- suppliers;
- a Chart of Accounts;
- no inventory in the previous file.

### 6.5 Bank and Credit Accounts

The business has three financial accounts to connect or configure:

- one cheque account;
- one GST account;
- one credit card.

### 6.6 Operational Requirements

The company also needs to:

- customize customer invoices;
- track bills;
- support job allocation;
- introduce inventory tracking as the business grows.

### 6.7 Requirements Summary

| Business requirement | Relevant QuickBooks area |
|---|---|
| Quarterly BAS and monthly IAS | GST Centre and tax reporting |
| PAYG instalments | Tax settings and activity statements |
| Employee payroll | Payroll setup and pay runs |
| Contractor reporting | TPARS |
| Multiple internal and professional users | Manage Users and accountant access |
| Migration from another system | Import data and opening balances |
| Existing customers and suppliers | Customer and supplier imports |
| Existing Chart of Accounts | Chart of Accounts import |
| Bank and credit-card accounts | Banking Centre and reconciliation |
| Customized invoices | Custom Form Styles |
| Bill tracking | Bills and supplier payments |
| Job allocation | Projects, classes, or related tracking features |
| Inventory growth | Products, services, and inventory tracking |

## 7. Implementation Lessons for an Internal Accounting System

The introductory material suggests several design principles that are useful beyond QuickBooks itself.

1. **Match the system to the business model.** Project, appointment, service, retail, and inventory businesses need different workflows.
2. **Plan user roles before rollout.** Owners, employees, accountants, bookkeepers, and external agents should not automatically receive identical permissions.
3. **Treat migration as a separate workstream.** Customers, suppliers, accounts, opening balances, invoices, bills, and bank data require validation and sequencing.
4. **Confirm regional compliance.** Tax, payroll, banking, and reporting features are country-specific.
5. **Support mobile access without weakening controls.** Convenience must be balanced with authentication, authorization, audit, and device security.
6. **Document the target operating model.** The training case works because tax frequency, users, accounts, migration data, and future requirements are stated before setup begins.
